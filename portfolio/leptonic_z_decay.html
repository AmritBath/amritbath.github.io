Investigating the Leptonic Decay of the Z Boson

This project focused on analysing ATLAS detector data from the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) to measure the mass of the Z boson via its leptonic decay channel. Using ROOT and Python, I applied selection cuts to isolate muon pairs and reconstruct their invariant mass.

I fit a Gaussian curve to the resulting distribution and evaluated experimental uncertainties. The final result closely aligned with the accepted Z boson mass, reinforcing the reliability of our data selection and fitting approach.

Through this work, I gained experience in data filtering, histogramming, statistical fitting, and interpretation of high-energy physics datasets.
