Timing the Crab Pulsar – Jodrell Bank

In this project, I conducted high-precision pulsar timing using radio observations of the Crab Pulsar from Jodrell Bank Observatory. I implemented algorithms to correct for barycentric motion, extract pulse times-of-arrival (TOAs), and compute residuals.

The analysis involved fitting frequency and spin-down models to the data, identifying systematic errors in the residuals, and interpreting the pulsar's period evolution. I also explored orthogonal distance regression (ODR) to refine gradient estimation and uncertainty propagation.

This was a technically rigorous project that strengthened my astrophysical data analysis skills, particularly in time-domain studies and signal interpretation.
