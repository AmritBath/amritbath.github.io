Social Media & Loneliness in Adolescents

This essay critically explores the paradox of social connection through always-on social media use, focusing on adolescents. While digital platforms promise belonging and community, their always-available nature often generates performative pressures, identity fragmentation, and emotional exhaustion.

I examined psychological literature on loneliness, structural critiques of platform capitalism, and cultural references like Euphoria and the Metaverse. The piece argues that digital engagement is not inherently isolating, but is shaped by design choices that amplify emotional labour and algorithmic conformity.
